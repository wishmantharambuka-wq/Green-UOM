Welcome to the UoM GreenMap

Our Campus, Our Green Lung

The University of Moratuwa is a hub of innovation and intellect, uniquely positioned within a dense urban landscape. As our city grows, the pressure on natural spaces increases. This project explores our campus's most valuable, living asset: its trees.

More than just scenery, our campus trees function as a vital "green lung." They actively combat air pollution, mitigate the urban heat island effect, manage stormwater, and provide quiet, shaded spaces for learning and collaboration. In an urbanized area, this green infrastructure is not a luxuryâ€”it is a necessity.

An Urban Informatics Perspective

This GreenMap is an Urban Informatics initiative to see the campus differently. Instead of just seeing trees, we see a distributed network of environmental assets. We are mapping, measuring (using data like Height and DBH), and analyzing our green canopy to understand its true impact.
